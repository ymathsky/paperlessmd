## CHAPTER 4 — Starting a Visit Form — Step by Step (~8 min)

### Intro slate
> Chapter title: "Chapter 4: The Visit Form — Full Walkthrough"

---

**[SAY]**
"The visit form is the core workflow of PaperlessMD. I'm going to walk through every step so you know exactly what to expect."

**[ACTION]** From Betty Johnson's profile, click **New Form** → **Vital CS**.

### Step 0 — Visit Info
**[SAY]**
"Step zero is visit info. Select the provider — start typing a name and it autocompletes from your staff list. The date defaults to today but you can change it for backdated entries. Pick the visit type — New, Follow Up, Sick, or Post Hospital — and the time auto-fills with right now."

**[ACTION]** Point to the progress bar.

**[SAY]**
"Notice the progress bar at the top. Eight steps, always shows exactly where you are."

### Step 1 — Chief Complaint & ICD-10
**[SAY]**
"Step one is the chief complaint and ICD-10 codes. I'll use the microphone button to show voice dictation — this works on any text field in any form."

**[ACTION]** Click the mic icon, say "Sacral wound stage two with serosanguineous drainage."

**[SAY]**
"The text transcribes in real time. Now I'll search for the matching ICD-10 code."

**[ACTION]** Type "sacral pressure" in the ICD-10 search box, click code L89.152.

**[SAY]**
"The code becomes a chip above the search box. You can add up to six codes. Want the AI to suggest more? Click AI Suggest."

**[ACTION]** Click AI Suggest, show the response.

**[SAY]**
"The AI reads the chief complaint and recommends additional codes with its reasoning. Click any suggestion to add it. Remove a code by clicking the X on the chip."

### Step 2 — Vitals
**[SAY]**
"Step two — vitals. Click the Blood Pressure card and the numpad slides in."

**[ACTION]** Click BP card, tap a preset like 120/80.

**[SAY]**
"Tap a preset and it fills automatically, then advances to the next vital. For a stable patient you can tap Set Normal Values to fill everything in one shot — blood pressure, pulse, temperature, oxygen saturation, respiratory rate — all in under five seconds."

**[ACTION]** Click Set Normal Values, show all vitals filling.

**[SAY]**
"Weight and height are at the bottom — BMI calculates automatically."

### Step 3 — Medications
**[SAY]**
"Step three — medications. Notice the pre-filled rows highlighted in amber. These came from Betty's master medication list automatically. The MA doesn't type a single medication name."

**[ACTION]** Click a frequency field, show the quick-pills (QD/BID/TID/etc.).

**[SAY]**
"Tap a frequency pill to fill the field instantly. The Type dropdown lets you mark each med as Maintenance, Refill, New prescription, or Discontinued."

### Step 4 — Allergies & Homebound
**[SAY]**
"Step four — allergies and homebound status. Type any allergies in the free text box. Then check the homebound reason boxes — these are the CMS criteria your billing team needs."

### Steps 5–7 — Consents
**[SAY]**
"Steps five through seven are the consent forms — CCM consent, ABN, and additional authorizations. These are pre-populated with the patient's information and require the patient's acknowledgment before the signature step."

### Step 8 — Signatures
**[SAY]**
"Step eight — signatures. This is where everything gets locked in."

**[ACTION]** Show the patient signature canvas.

**[SAY]**
"The patient signs here on the canvas — with a finger on a tablet or a mouse on a laptop. If a guardian is signing, flip the POA toggle and fields appear for the guardian's name and relationship."

**[ACTION]** Show the MA signature block with the auto-fill badge.

**[SAY]**
"The MA signature block — if the MA has saved their signature in their profile, you'll see the Auto-fill ON badge and the signature draws automatically. They can toggle it off to sign manually for any individual form."

**[ACTION]** Click **Submit & Save**.

**[SAY]**
"Click Submit and Save. The form is signed, saved to Betty's record, and appears in the provider's sign queue instantly. No paper. No scanning. No uploading later."
